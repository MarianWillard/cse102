CSE 102 NAND

NAND is NOT AND, meaning, the output of AND put through a NOT. Thus, NAND is 1 if either or both of the inputs is 0. With this understanding, NAND gates can be used to make any of the other gates.

AND Circuit

To make an AND, we see that NAND returns 0 of both inputs are 1. The first two NANDs output 1 if A or B is 0. The right NAND then takes two inputs of 1 and returns 0. When A and B are 1, the first two NANDs output 0, which causes the right NAND to output 1. This equates an AND gate.

OR Circuit

To make an OR, we simply uncross the A and B inputs from the previous circuit diagram, thus A and B are used as both inputs for the first two NANDs. The right NAND outputs 0 only if both its inputs are 1. The first two NANDs output 1 only if A and B are both 0. Otherwise, the circuit output is 1.

NOT Circuit

Making a NOT using NANDs is surprisingly simple. NANDs output 0 when both inputs are 1 and 1 when both inputs are 0. That is exactly a NOT only requiring two inputs.

XOR Circuit

XOR is exclusive or, meaning output 1 only if one input is 1. To do this with NANDs, we start with our OR circuit, and add a third NAND at the front. A and B are then put through that first NAND and then into the middle two NANDs along side the A and B inputs.
This means that when A and B are 1, the first NAND outputs 0, causing the middle two NANDs to output 1, which in turn causes teh last NAND to output 0. Thus, the circuit outputs 0 when A and B are both 1.
